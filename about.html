I grew up in the San Fernando Valley during the 90's. Kurt Cobain was a fading memory and music and culture had a life that one doesn't find these days. The multiverse perpetuated by modern day technology and algorithms was at best the fantasy of some autocratic tech bussiness man child.






On ART
Despite being rather musically inclinded-listening to music all alone in private as a child and starting up my first instrument in middle school-I never really considered myself artistic before a couple of years ago. This is in muted contrast to the fact that I thought of myself as a right brained person, because I enjoyed reading books slightly more than the average peer. It started off by workplace boredom (something which, given my current lack of employment sounds great) that I sought to fight off without using my phone. I was working at a school helping to watch over troublesome teens. One teacher had a particularly firm grasp on her little hoodlums. This left me to my own devices for large swathes of my time in her classroom and it wasn't before long that I decied to grab a piece of scrap paper and just doodle away. I'd never really doodled as a young person. That isn't to say I never tried. It just never worked out and (along with my horrific handwriting) I reasoned I wasn't one of those kids that can draw. Anyway, it went pretty bad but it was mildly amusing and so it became a habit that due to my neurotic nature became a real HABIT. It wasn't long, perhaps a day or two before I figured i'd come in with a photo on my phone (the original thing I was trying to avoid) that I wanted to draw. The kids thought it looked close-ish and I gained points with them. THose points were motivation and that quickly became a motivation to improve my drawing... It took a while but eventually I started to realize that I wasn't really in my usual state of mind, as I drew.
